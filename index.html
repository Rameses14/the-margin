Why We Can’t Stop Re-Watching the Things We’ve Already Seen

On comfort, nostalgia, and the quiet rebellion of the rewind button — and what our re-runs say about us.

There is a particular kind of evening when nothing new will do. The algorithm’s endless buffet feels less like abundance than like a dare, and the only honest answer is to return, once more, to a story whose ending we already know by heart. We are, all of us, secret re-watchers — and there is more wisdom in the habit than we admit.
