There is always a poster of Mars: a red sky, a figure in a white suit on a dune, a rocket behind them. It is the picture we have all agreed means the future. But there is one thing the poster never shows you — somebody, inside that shiny dome, has to do the dishes.

We have been sold the wrong story about Mars. It is all adventure — plant the flag, take the photograph. But getting there is almost the easy part. The hard problem is staying alive once you arrive, with no deliveries and no working tap, for years at a time.

The frontier, it turns out, is a plumbing problem.

Take water, the whole game. You cannot carry enough of it, so the only way the maths works is to use the same water again, and again, and again. (Continue writing…)
