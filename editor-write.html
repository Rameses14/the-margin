Pop Culture

Somewhere between thirteen and seventeen, the world stops asking you to be quiet and starts asking you to have an opinion — loudly, and preferably online. This is the volume knob of adolescence, and nobody hands you the manual.

What interests me is not the noise itself but the way we’ve started mistaking it for substance. A take is not a thought. A repost is not a belief. And yet the louder the channel, the more certain we are that someone, somewhere, is finally listening.

We were promised a megaphone. What we got was a room where everyone is shouting and no one can hear.

I want to make the unfashionable case for turning it down. Not off — down. There is a register, just above silence, where you can actually think. (Continue writing…)
