Issue No. 2 — “Noise”

Theme: the sound the world makes, and what we miss when we stop listening.
In planning · 4 of 5 briefed
The throughline. Every piece this issue turns on a different kind of noise — social, civic, comic, competitive, cinematic. The issue should build from the personal (a teenager’s feed) outward to the public (a city that never quiets), then let the lighter pieces breathe. We want a mix of one strong argument per writer, not balanced both-sides reporting — this is an opinion magazine.

Pop Culture

The Volume Knob of Adolescence

Ananya Reddy · Grade 11 · In review
Edit brief
The plan — subject · angle · what’s discussed A first-person essay on growing up in a culture that first tells you to be quiet, then demands you have loud opinions online. Ananya’s argument: we’ve started mistaking volume for substance — a take is not a thought, a repost is not a belief — and the most interesting register is the one just above silence, where you can actually think. Discusses social-media performativity, the pressure to “have a position,” and a modest defence of turning the noise down rather than off.
Science

What Silence Costs a City

Sara Iqbal · Grade 10 · Draft
Edit brief
The plan — subject · angle · what’s discussed A reported science essay on noise pollution and the measurable toll of a city that never goes quiet. Sara’s argument: silence is an undervalued public good we’re losing without noticing, and we should treat it as infrastructure, not luxury. Discusses the research on noise and sleep, stress and concentration, why hospitals and schools suffer most, and what quieter urban design would actually look like.
Sports

The Last Bench, Revisited

Arjun Nair · Grade 11 · Scheduled
Edit brief
The plan — subject · angle · what’s discussed A piece on the substitutes’ bench as the real social and emotional core of a school team — the noise of the sideline. Arjun’s argument: belonging, effort and quiet loyalty matter more than minutes played, and a team is best judged by how it treats the kid who rarely gets on. Discusses bench culture, the dignity of showing up, and why we over-celebrate the scoreboard.
Humour

A Field Guide to Corridor Acoustics

Meher Joshi · Grade 9 · Draft
Edit brief
The plan — subject · angle · what’s discussed A comic mock-taxonomy of the noises of a school corridor between periods. Meher’s angle: affectionate, observational humour — the hallway as a soundscape with its own identifiable species (the slammed-locker, the migrating friend-group, the lone whistler). Discusses, with a straight face, the “ecology” of school din. Keeps it light; this is the issue’s palate-cleanser.
Arts · Film

The Case Against the Quiet Movie

Nikhil Rao · Grade 10 · Pitch
Edit brief
The plan — subject · angle · what’s discussed A contrarian film essay defending loud, maximalist cinema against the prestige of the hushed, “tasteful” quiet film. Nikhil’s argument: some stories earn their volume, and our reflex to equate restraint with quality is its own kind of snobbery. Discusses sound design as storytelling, spectacle versus minimalism, and a few films that are great precisely because they refuse to whisper.
